Every argument carries its own **rating market** — a constant-product pair of the argument's *pro* and *con* reserves. The market is the mechanism behind [rating](rating), the debate's incentives, and its moderation. An argument's **approval** is its pro-share price, derived from the reserve balance.

## Seeding

An argument's market is born with the argument itself: the author's chosen [deposit](editing) becomes the market's initial pro/con reserves, split at the author's **initial approval** — their opening claim of how strongly the argument holds. The deposit is sunk into the reserves: it subsidizes the raters who correct the rating, and it sets the argument's starting weight.

## Staking

During the [Rating phase](rating), participants stake [vote tokens](tokens) on a market's pro or con side. Each stake

- pays a small **market fee** (5%) that accrues to the argument's **author** — the authorship incentive for arguments that attract rating volume, claimable once the debate is finished,
- swaps against the market's current reserves under constant-product pricing, so a side's price rises as more of it is demanded, and
- adds the staked tokens to the argument's **weight** — the quantity that determines how heavily the argument counts against its siblings at [tallying](tallying).

## Redemption

Once the debate is finished, shareholders redeem their shares at the market's **final approval price**. Staking the way the crowd ultimately agreed — especially *early*, when shares were cheap — pays; staking against the final consensus costs. The payout rule is provably solvent: the reserves always cover every redemption.

## Why markets?

A plain vote count measures how many people clicked, not how much they mean it or how carefully they judged. A market makes every rating a priced commitment: overrated arguments become expensive to inflate further and **profitable to bet against** — which is also deliberate's moderation. Spam and manipulation are not adjudicated by a court; they are corrected for profit by other participants. There are deliberately no disputes, curators, or arbitrators.
